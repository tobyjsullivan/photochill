How It Works/The Small Print

In order to provide the best possible service for keeping your RAW photos, we use a special storage mechanism that has some interesting characteristics you'll probably want to know about.

Thawed Photos

When you upload a photo, we'll keep it "thawed out" and readily available.

This doesn't mean much other than you can download that photo anytime you want without delay. However, this method of storage is expensive and we want you to be able to store as many photos as possible so we only keep your most recent photos (300 for the Hobbiest plan) thawed. After that, we start "freezing" your photos.

Frozen Photos

In order to let your store tens of thousands of photos (30,000 for the Hobbiest plan), we "freeze" all your photos other than your most recent few hundred. This means your photos need to "thaw out" before you can get them.

Once you've decided you want to use an old RAW photo that's currently frozen, you come to RAW Chill and tell us to thaw it. After about five hours, we'll email you and let you know that your photo is thawed and ready for download.

Upload Speeds

On a slightly more technical note, RAW Chill is based on the requirement that you upload large files. To ensure maximum satisfaction with our service, we recommend you have an internet plan that provides you with a minimum of 2Mbps "upload" speed. If you are not sure, you can call your internet service provider to determine what you have or how you can get this. RAW Chill will still work on slower connections, it might just be a bit slow.
